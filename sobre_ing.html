What it is?

The flip-flop, also called slipper, underlay or Flat Sandals, is a type of sandal typically used as a form of article of clothing. It consists of a flat sole held loosely on the foot by a Y shaped strap that passes between the first and second toes and around both sides of the foot or can be a hard base with a strip around all fingers.

History

The first evidence of the use of flip-flop was in Egypt, in the year 4000 BC, made of papyrus, fibers or straw fastened to the feet by strips of raw leather. They used them to protect their feet on scorching ground, tan the top of their feet and women for the application of jewelry and props.

Why is that name?

The term flip-flop has been used in American and British English since the 1960s to describe inexpensive footwear consisting of a flat base, typically rubber, and a strap with three anchor points: between the big and second toes, then bifurcating to anchor on both sides of the foot. "Flip-flop" may be an onomatopoeia of the sound made by the sandals when walking in them.
